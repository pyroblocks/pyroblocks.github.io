RAE's Raleigh Open #6 and Major Odds (Week Two)

Who will deliver in the final regional of the season?

May 27, 2025

It's time to send our final teams to Raleigh.

Last week, NRG, SSG, Gen.G, Ultimates, Falcons, Twisted Minds, Wildcard, TSM, and FUT Esports cemented their spots at both the Raleigh Major and the World Championship. This week, another seven teams will join them.

NA/MENA/OCE/SSA Recap

Here's how the RAE model did last week.

In terms of major spots, North America went about as expected, with The Ultimates locking up the final berth after Spacestation and Gen.G both did so on Friday. SSG's win was unexpected, but not catastrophic for the model: It still predicted 79% of series correctly, and they've since moved up to #2 in North America in the model's eyes.

MENA was highly predictable once again, with Falcons (despite their underperformance for most of the split) finding their way out of yet another Game 7 jam and taking the regional triple crown over Twisted Minds. The Vicious getting dropped in round one of the GSL stage meant that they knocked ROC out before the playoffs, which killed off most of the excitement fairly early.

Unfortunately for PWR, they pulled off an exact repeat of the first split by losing right before the final hurdle to Chiefs. TSM's do-or-die run managed to clinch them a spot in Raleigh and Lyon in one fell swoop, but they'll be the lowest rated team at the major. Time will tell if they can hold onto this form. RAE went a pedestrian 75% in OCE.

SSA once again was highly unpredictable, though after accounting for the smurfs on traduis si tu pues, it managed to scrape a similar 75% accuracy. Limitless failed to make a single grand finals this split; the region is in dire straits at this point.

Overall, the model had an accuracy of 79% last weekend.

Europe

Karmine have finally settled at a rating of around 2100 after consistent increases throughout the season. With Vitality's struggles and their Open #5 victory, Dignitas are starting to close the gap for the EU2 spot.

Let's go over some of the more controversial picks.

What's going on with the 4–6 range?

Most people have EU's 4th through 6th best teams ranked as Gentle Mates, Geekay, and Ninjas, in that order. RAE disagrees.

Geekay, whom RAE has always rated a step above where most people have them at, have stayed around the 1800 mark since the Birmingham Major, while Gentle Mates have had to slowly catch up, meaning that their rise hasn't quite been enough to pass Geekay quite yet.

As for Ninjas, they've also coasted since the major, meaning that they've stayed below Geekay and above Mates. They've largely suffered from a discrepancy in their results and their quality — they can't stop running into good teams.

NIP's average opponent rating in the GSL stage across Open #4 and Open #5 has been 1771, compared to 1513 for Geekay and 1579 for Gentle Mates. They've had few opportunities to beat up on bad teams and gain rating, so instead have been stuck running in place against the likes of KCorp and Dignitas.

The Ninjas could very well be as good as RAE says they are. They finally dodged their problem matchups and will only have to beat CALIENTE and Geekay to make it through uppers this time around — though it might be too little too late. They need to make the grand finals to even have a realistic shot of booking a ticket to Raleigh.

How is Team BSK the 15th best team in the tournament?

Once again, it's a story of RAE being cautious not to overreact to one good result.

To be clear, it was one very good result. They took wins over a pair of teams in the top 8 bubble against Smokey Bacon Association and F4WD before coming extremely close to taking down Vitality in the upper bracket.

Still, their talent composite meant that they started Open #5 extremely low, and though they gained an impressive 75 rating in the event, that still leaves them towards the bottom of the table. If they can follow up on their performance, they should find themselves in a more competitive position by the LCQ.

Karmine and Dignitas secured their spots at the major with their respective event wins. Vitality will likely join them by Saturday.

Geekay finds themselves as slight favorites over Gentle Mates for the last spot, though both teams will likely face important tests in the upper bracket of the GSL stage against Ninjas and Dignitas, respectively.

For Geekay especially, making it through the upper bracket will be important. If they fall to Ninjas, a date against either Synergy, BSK, or BDS awaits.

Though a handful of teams are tied with them on points, NIP's advantage in quality gives them a slight chance to make a miracle run and do to Geekay what Geekay did to them in the first split. At the very least, they have twice the odds of doing so than GK did when they pulled it off.

South America

FURIA and Secret are the clear top two teams, but the gap between Corinthians and their counterparts has begun to shrink. Both Amethyst and the newly christened Kovya Godfidence will be nipping at their heels for the SAM3 spot, with not much else on the line for any team.

How competitive is the SAM LCQ going to be?

If we thought it was going to be close before, it's going to be an absolute dogfight now.

Though Corinthians' addition of Reysbull, buoyed their rating slightly, they've struggled to keep up their Birmingham form, and now find themselves attempting to hold off both Amethyst and Godfidence after losing to each team in Open #5.

What we're seeing is yet another repetition of what happened to shad and ajg when they returned to South America after being kicked from Complexity. Reysbull is struggling to keep pace with the rest of the region at times, and it's allowed for their SAM3 spot to be dragged into question.

Even beyond the 3–5 range, TBS, Sunset, and Moonrise have each had their day where they were the third best team in the region, and will all be hoping for a stroke of luck to tilt things in their favor. It's going to come down to the wire.

Asia-Pacific

Virtus.pro and Movistar KOI are the clear 1-2 in the region, with KINOTROPE and Dreamstation making up the rest of the top 4. boba tea makes an appearance at #5 in their first full event as a team.

Can Virtus.pro repeat their Birmingham performance in Raleigh?

After putting APAC firmly on the map, the ex-Luminosity roster will doubtless be returning to the world stage — still underdogs, but with some expectations after going 2-3 in Birmingham.

If they can avoid any slip-ups, they'll be rated at #13 heading into the major, ahead of Wildcard, FUT, and TSM. The next highest team (probably Team Secret) will likely be a ways ahead of them, so they'll have their work cut out for them unless they can luck their way into another favorable bracket.

Regardless, this is the highest that an APAC team has ever been in RAE, both in terms of rating and ranking. It'll be interesting to see if they — and the region — can build off of this performance.
