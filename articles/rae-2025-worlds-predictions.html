RAE Predictions — 2025 World Championship

Who will rise to the summit of the RLCS?

August 29, 2025

We've finally made it.

After a nine month-long regular season filled with triumph, tribulation, heartbreak, and cinematic moments aplenty, we've reached the final stop on our journey: Lyon.

20 teams enter the LDLC arena with hopes of claiming the most coveted reward Rocket League has to offer: the World Championship trophy. Only one of them will take it home.

If you'd like an explanation of what exactly RAE is, you can find one here.

EU LCQ Recap

Similar to North America's, RAE had difficulty predicting Europe's Last-Chance Qualifier. At least in EU's case, though, it was more unpredictability than a poor performance by the model.

Out of the eight mistakes it made while predicting the event, three can be attributed to Team BDS finally playing up to their level, a first for them after a dismal Split 2. Two can be credited to CALIENTE's roller coaster Day 1 (which saw them take down Hogan Mode before getting knocked out by Sick! in lowers), with another two coming at the hands of a massive underperformance by German Amigos after a 3–0 run in Swiss.

The final upset was, of course, NiP's dismantling of Gentle Mates, which eventually led to them securing their region's spot in Lyon.

Overall, RAE clocked an accuracy of just 71% in Europe. Sometimes days like these just happen.

RAE Rankings — 2025 World Championship

After their EWC win, Karmine Corp are the slight favorites over Team Falcons, with Vitality in a clear third and Dignitas rounding out the top four.

Beyond the top four, from teams ranked 5–13, every team is within 25 rating of the team below it (besides for the gap between Team Secret and Ninjas). Given the instability, inconsistency, and high peaks of most of the teams in this range, don't be surprised if Lyon is full of upsets like the ones we saw in Raleigh.

Geekay have moved from 10th to 6th after their EWC performance, but will be thrust right back into the action with a round one matchup against an Ultimates team looking for revenge.

After their disaster in Raleigh and nightmare against Vitality, Spacestation have recovered their rating to where they now have a reasonable shot at sneaking into the top 8. As the bracket's set up, it will be Twisted Minds that will attempt to deny them yet another upset win.

Who's going to make it out of the Play-In?

New to Worlds this year is the Play-In, where the bottom four teams that qualified directly to the event face off against the four teams that made it through the LCQs.

It just so happens that 7/8 of these teams are among RAE's worst eight rated, with only Ninjas in Pyjamas sitting in the top 12. Thanks to some, uh, "clever" seeding, Wildcard managed to scrape the 12th seed over Gen.G and Virtus.pro, allowing them to dodge the first day of the tournament. RAE still rates them as the 17th best team here, though, and they'll likely struggle against whoever they face after their probable loss to FURIA.

Thanks to a combination of being the clear best team in the Play-In and dodging the other two upper-echelon teams, NiP sits with the best odds to escape the Play-In (83%). Likely to join them are Gen.G and ROC, though at least one of them will have to go through lowers, as they are projected to play each other in the upper qualifier match.

Hovering on the bubble are MIBR and Virtus.pro. The latter has the more friendly first round match, and there's a solid chance they play against each other to qualify as a result. MIBR is rated as the better team, though, and if they pull off an upset over Gen.G, the entire bracket could get scrambled.

As to be expected, FUT and TSM both have their work cut out for them. Out of the two, TSM at least has a nicer bracket to work with after facing NiP, but most likely they'll both be going out dead last.


...alright, let's talk about Shopify.

Shopify at EIGHTEEN???

For those of you used to my once-per-LAN explainer of why North America is "underrated," don't worry. It's coming.

But in Shopify's case, having them only above FUT and TSM seems very low. I agree, but this is a team that has had a total of one good event. If you read my NA LCQ explainer, you'd know that everything below the NA5 position has been hot garbage in the second split. Shopify, starting from a rating of 1455 before the event, really only put together convincing wins in half of their series. Thus, they only gained 80 rating.

To be clear, 80 rating points is nothing to sneeze at. But when you're starting from that low, it's not enough to move you to a spot where RAE feels you're all that competitive with international competition. Honestly, knowing this team's propensity for inconsistency, RAE may very well be showing their accuracy level.

That being said, if they do what's expected of them and at least beat Virtus.pro, they'll rise to a more reasonable spot. It depends on how the rest of NA does, but realistically their ceiling is pretty low to begin with, so getting close to MIBR is probably a good enough result for them.

Is North America actually this bad?

Honestly? Yeah.

NRG have been midding it up this entire season. Ultimates can't string together two consecutive good days of Rocket League. SSG can't string together five consecutive minutes of holding their controllers. Gen.G just can't in general.

The only really debatable points are Gen.G being below ROC, Ultimates being below FURIA, and NRG and SSG both being a tad too low.

Gen.G have been pretty unimpressive for most of the season, and for those of you who missed the MENA LCQ, ROC looked unbelievably good. This ranking is completely defensible, and when that's the case, I tend not to put my thumb on the scale.

The Ultimates case is more a result of FURIA being the stickiest team I've ever seen in RAE. They keep sitting higher than they should be, but to be fair it's also important not to overreact to one or two event results. As always, things will probably work themselves out. I hope. FURIA's bracket is a much bigger influence on their higher playoff odds than being slightly higher than Ultimates in rating.

NRG struggle to take big wins over good teams, so their rating tends to be easier to move down than up. As a result, they're sitting at around 40 points behind Dignitas, when realistically I think most would agree that they should be higher than that. That being said, until they start beating the top 4 instead of just playing them close™, they'll continue to be lower than we think they should be. At the very least, their head-to-head matchups against everyone above them besides for Vitality seem to be pretty good, so a win like that is bound to happen eventually. Right?

As for SSG... they played about one good series. Them still being a good margin below a Twisted Minds team that has been top 8 in the world for the entire season is the right decision for the model to make here.

Look, I'm sure you're as tired of reading these as I am of writing them. NA is an underwhelming region, even when they have what's on paper a good event. When they start winning, and start winning convincingly, then their rating will reflect how good you think they are.

With all of that being said, NRG is still winning Worlds.
