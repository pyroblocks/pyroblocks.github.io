Birmingham Major RAE Predictions

Who does RAE predict to raise the trophy?

March 12, 2025

The stage is set for the sixteen teams flying out to Birmingham for the first RLCS Major of 2025.

Which of them will call themselves champions at Sunday's end?

If you'd like a detailed explanation of what exactly RAE is, you can find one here.

NA/MENA/OCE/SSA Recap

Here's a brief summary of how the RAE model did in each region last week.

In North America, RAE got 3/4 major teams correct, missing, like everyone, Gen.G (it predicted 9Lives, then Spacestation, to make it to Birmingham instead). Gen.G's run, combined with a poor performance by NRG, dropped the event accuracy to 67%, by far RAE's worst performance of the season. SSG and their inconsistency continue to plague the RAE model.

MENA, historically one of the most top-heavy regions in the RLCS, returned to normalcy with RAE predicting 81% of matches correctly. The model called Falcons and Twisted Minds clinching as well, though it would've taken a monumental series of events for one of the two teams to miss the major. sleeping's surprise run to the top 4 helped to reinforce MENA's depth: With four teams within 7 points of the region's third seed and another three showing high potential right below them, it's going to be a dogfight in the region's likely Last Chance Qualifier.

RAE rebounded in OCE, following up it's then-worst event of the season (69% accuracy) with its best (88%). Only 2man upsetting Prodigy really catches the eye, as otherwise the region's six best teams would've made up the entirety of Championship Sunday, and finished in their power-ranked order.

Despite three-straight FUT-Limitless grand finals, SSA has had some of the most chaos of any region this season, and this event was no different. Mustu Fluck and 77Blocks, rated as SSA's fourth and fifth best teams entering the event, respectively, went out dead last, while Death Cloud Esports scrambled the bracket with a win versus Bulletproof (who would proceed to finish with a top 4).

RAE accumulated a 77% prediction accuracy this week, a tick up from the 75% from the last go around. The major will be the real test of the model's rankings, though, as international matchups are rare compared to the host of intra-region data it receives.

Birmingham Major RAE Rankings

I'm going to preface the entire rest of this article by saying that I was dreading the rankings fallout from NRG's mediocre Open #3. RAE hates North America in comparison to Europe, and it prefers the greater consistency out of FURIA, Twisted Minds, and Falcons in their minor regions, even if they are facing weaker competition. That, and it really loves Geekay thanks to their talent and 12-0 Sunday in Open #3.

Let's go over some of the more controversial picks.

NA is Washed????

The general consensus across the RL scene is that NRG is, if not the second best team in the world, very close to it, and one of the favorites for the major. RAE disagrees: While NA's strongest warriors would only be small underdogs in any given matchup, it would still favor teams like Geekay and Twisted Minds over them.

The result? Thanks to their general inconsistency in Open #3 and taking a trio of ugly losses to a good-but-not-great SSG team, RAE rates NRG as the 7th best team in Birmingham, giving them only a 4% chance of winning.

I don't think there should be too much disagreement about the placement of NA's other three major squads; Ultimates and Complexity could be a little higher, but at least in COL's case I am personally convinced they are destined to go 2-3 regardless of how good they play and thus their rating doesn't actually matter. Ditto for Wildcard and Gen.G.

Still, RAE thinks this is the worst NA's looked in comparison to the rest of the world since Worlds 22-23. As long as the region's third and fourth seeds keep putting up poor performances, they will continue to be rated a step below EU and the very top of SAM and MENA unless they have a single squad that is dominating the field. At the very least, NRG should gain throughout the event as long as they can recapture their 2024 form.

Can anybody catch Karmine?

Karmine Corp are the runaway favorites for the entire event. This should be neither surprising nor controversial, but RAE is especially bullish on them: It gives the Blue Wall a greater chance of winning than it gives Vitality to make the semifinals.

I certainly wouldn't call anyone crazy for picking KC not to win, but they will need to be toppled before you can even start to say that another team is more likely to lift the trophy than them. They only lost one series all split (an 0-4 against Geekay), and if they won it, RAE would probably say they were more likely to come away with the trophy than not.

Still, Karmine are approaching a level of domination not seen since... Karmine Corp before Copenhagen last season. Maybe we shouldn't crown them champions just yet.

Will a minor region team finally win an RLCS LAN?

If it was going to happen at any time, this would probably be the event. Falcons are still in the same top form that they were in last season, but they've been joined at the top of MENA by Twisted Minds, who are a popular dark horse to pull off a few upsets and claim the trophy themselves.

FURIA have somehow become even more dominant in South America, not dropping a single series to this point in the season and only struggling against Team Secret, who could very well make it to playoffs themselves after only dropping series to FURIA (and one against Reds) all split.

Combine this with the best two squads OCE has ever sent (although it's watered down by the strength of the rest of the field) and a Luminosity team flying under the radar, and you have a recipe for the strongest top-to-bottom RLCS event of all time.

All in all, minor regions have a combined 31% chance to win. History could very well be made.

I also want to note that top 8 bubble teams have their odds significantly boosted compared to last season, as the round you are coming from no longer matters in seeding swiss. In other words, two 1-2 teams could play for a spot in playoffs. Maybe Wildcard can finally break through. Here's hoping that 10th time's the charm for Torsos.
