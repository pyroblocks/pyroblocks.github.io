Raleigh Major RAE Rankings

Try as I might, by the end of Open #6, FURIA and Falcons both weaseled their way to the #2 and #3 spots once again ahead of the major. They're certainly close to them, and the gap between 2nd and 6th is almost negligible, but people tend to just look at the order of the teams, and NRG not being second is a pretty clear disagreement with the community's consensus.

At least North America seems to be more fairly rated this time around. You can thank RAE v1.5 for that.

FURIA and Falcons top 3? Still?

NRG had been sitting in front of both teams for most of the split, but they got passed by both at the last minute.

To be clear, RAE no longer thinks that NA is as good as MENA or SAM: By virtue of playing in the stronger region, NRG comes out with a 125- and over 200-rating headstart, respectively, over the titans of the other two regions (compared to -10 and 80 using the old region rating).

Still, NRG continues to have the odd strange performance, and while most people will count a 4-1 loss to Ultimates as a similar performance to getting taken to game 7 by Team Vision, the former lost NRG 32 rating, while the latter only lost Falcons 9. It also doesn't help that FURIA had one of the most dominant event performances of all time in SAM in Open #6, only dropping one game to Corinthians after sweeping TBS and Secret twice.

Heading into worlds, things should finally work themselves out, though another mediocre swiss performance from the Americans could see them as low as 6th at the end of the day.

Can anybody catch Karmine: Take 2

If Karmine were "runaway favorites" before Birmingham, then at this point they're about as close to winning as you can be before having played a single game.

Once again, they only dropped one series all split (that RAE tracks, so excluding Swiss): A 3-4 against Dignitas in the Open #5 grand finals. It cannot be understated just how dominant they've been, and anyone predicting Twisted Minds to take more than a game off of them should be called out for being unrealistic.

It's genuinely more likely that Gen.G makes top 8 than Karmine don't win. If they go 0-3, they might still be ranked #1 after the event. They're that far ahead of everyone else. Maybe they'll run into Wildcard again.

Will Gentle Mates claim EU's final worlds spot?

This shouldn't come as a surprise, but it's exceedingly unlikely.

Mates had a solid split, but failed to gain much on Geekay before making it to Raleigh, leaving a lot of work to be done to avoid having to play in the LCQ.

They need to make it to the grand finals to clinch the EU4 spot for worlds. Their probability of doing so? Just 3.6%. The org beat steeper odds to win the Copenhagen Major last year, so you never know, but the field is just so crowded this time around that it's a tough ask.
