Who will North America send to Lyon?

July 31, 2025

With the main RLCS 2025 circuit done and dusted, it's time for the best four regions to decide which four teams will secure their ticket to the World Championship in Lyon.

Third up: NA.

SAM LCQ Recap

South America broadly followed the script, with only a slight overperformance by Overlooked giving the model some trouble. The clear eight best teams made the top 8, with the best team winning, the top two teams making the grand finals, and the top four teams making the semifinals.

At the end of the day, it was the favorites in Corinthians that booked their ticket to the World Championship, a result that's been expected since the start of the season.

Overall, RAE recovered with an accuracy of 81% in the SAM LCQ, a slightly above-average performance for it this season.

RAE Rankings — NA LCQ

Complexity are the clear best team in the event, with a few teams (most notably Shopify, pepegashake, and The Boys) having an outside chance at pipping them to NA's final spot to Lyon.

hey bro, POAB, and CARL are all safely among the 8 best teams in the event, with the 8th place spot seeing a four-way brawl between NPL, G.A.S., Affinity, and 100X35.

Nothing here should be too surprising, except for maybe the high placement of pepegashake, who have quietly assembled a strong roster of leftovers from NA's top 8 bubble. We'll see if they can play up to their potential.

How big of a favorite is Complexity?

Complexity have easily been 5th best team in North America this season, with a sizeable gap between them and SSG for 4th as well as an even larger one between them and whichever NA team decides to play up to the NA6 ranking on any given matchday.

After the falloff of 9Lives, NA has struggled to find a clear 6th best team, with Deleted Gaming filling that spot in RAE's eyes by the end of Split 2.

As a result of NA's inconsistency, COL have set themself ahead as clear favorites, with them winning the event in a majority of RAE's 1,000,000 simulations.

The results on the pitch back this up, too: they've only dropped a single series to a team outside of NA's big 4 (to Schnitzelhaus in the Swiss Stage of Open #5). Assuming they can play at a level anywhere near their typical one, they should comfortably take the event win. It'll likely take a peaking Shopify or The Boys to knock them off their perch.
