RAE Predictions — 2025 EU LCQ

Who will Europe send to Lyon?

August 5, 2025

With the main RLCS 2025 circuit done and dusted, it's time for the best four regions to decide which four teams will secure their ticket to the World Championship in Lyon.

The final region sending a team to Worlds: Europe.

If you'd like a detailed explanation of what exactly RAE is, you can find one here.

NA LCQ Recap

Obviously, Complexity missing Lyon is the big result that catches the eye, but RAE struggled to accurately predict North America from start to finish in the LCQ. Despite the top 8 being full of 7/8 of RAE's top teams (you can thank hey bro losing to 100X35 for that), the model only salvaged a 72% prediction accuracy, one of its worst results of the season.

NPL's strong performance gave the model trouble, alongside a slow start from The Boys. RAE's hot take that pepegashake would be as strong of a contender as The Boys was disproven quickly on Day 2, as well.

Europe features a similar number of low bubble teams, but it at least has more players who made main events this season, which should help it to a rebound performance this week. I hope.

RAE Rankings — EU LCQ

Gentle Mates are clear favorites, with Ninjas in Pyjamas the only other team within 250 rating points of them.

2 Danes 1 Bruv, Hogan Mode, and Team BSK comprise the top 4 bubble, while Red Devils, German Hermanos, CALIENTE, and Team BDS being the main teams in contention for the playoffs.

The rest of the top 16 is filled with low bubble rosters, though depending on if you think North America is better than RAE gives it credit for (which they probably are), then Low Block might be a bit underrated here.

Is it really down to Gentle Mates versus Ninjas?

Though COL's loss last week should show us that anything can happen, realistically, the race for Lyon should be won by one of these two squads.

Gentle Mates (#8 worldwide) and Ninjas in Pyjamas (#12) are the top two teams in the world playing in a Last-Chance Qualifier, and unlike most of the rest of the squads competing over these past few weeks, both of them could realistically find a way into the playoffs in Lyon. Hell, Mates already did as much in Raleigh.

Between these two, it's Gentle Mates' event to lose: most would agree that the Frenchmen are Europe's fourth best team by this point (ahead of Geekay), and though NiP owned the Split 1 head-to-head, it's since flipped in GM8's favor.

RAE rates bringing Radosin in to replace itachi a negative move for the Ninjas, dropping their rating down 60 points and turning what likely would've been 40% odds to 30% odds. An upset could very well still be on the cards — it's just unlikely that anyone besides them will be doing the upsetting. All in all, the two teams combined have over a 90% chance of winning the event.

Is Team BSK really fifth?

RAE is an Elo system, not a Glicko system, meaning that it's as quick to adjust to results from a new team like Team BSK as it is to a team that's been around for a while. So, they've had to drag their rating up from the default 1300 up to 1500 over the course of just three events this split.

They could very well be the third best team at this event, and based on their Split 2 form it's reasonable to expect them to be. With the rest of Europe's chase pack (100%, F4WD, and $ATM) splitting up, new rosters like 2 Danes 1 Bruv and the ever-unpredictable Hogan Mode will have questions to answer that BSK probably won't. We've already seen Dopshot, who would've been the sixth best team in the event, drop out early.

Hogan Mode could just clean sweep everyone, though. You never know.
