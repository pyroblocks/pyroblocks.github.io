RAE's Raleigh Open #6 and Major Odds (Week One)

Who will book their spot at the final major?

May 21, 2024

And after two months, we're back.

I apologize for the lack of a Birmingham debriefing; the major coincided with the end of my spring break, so I jumped right into the final stretch of the year and it didn't cross my mind to make one until it would've been way too late. Without further ado, though, let's see who RAE favors to make it to Raleigh out of NA, MENA, OCE, and SSA.

If you'd like an explanation of what exactly RAE is, you can find one here.

Birmingham Major Recap & RAE v1.5

Alright, I know I said that it would be too late to do one of these, but I might as well do my due diligence.

To cut to the chase, RAE had an accuracy of 74% at the major, which is neither great nor terrible. The Swiss was very chalky (with the exception of Luminosity pulling off a couple of upsets), but playoffs had just enough upsets to keep things at an average accuracy rating. LAN accuracy will always be lower than online accuracy due to having a smaller sample size, so 74% on RAE's first time out isn't bad.

As everyone expected, North America overperformed where RAE had them. To be fair, they outperformed everyone's expectations a bit (getting two teams in the top 4), but RAE thought that both NRG and The Ultimates would go out in the top 8, which they obviously didn't.

FURIA underperformed RAE's lofty expectations, but by the end of the event moved about to where most people had them: slightly higher than Vitality. RAE's high ratings for Team Falcons and Twisted Minds were both disproven (alongside Geekay, but they at least moved down to #8 in the post-event rankings). Overall, though the match accuracy was solid, RAE overrated minor regions and underrated major regions, and even after everything was said and done still had Falcons at #2, FURIA at #3, and both NA semifinalists at #6 and #7.

Even though the accuracy was alright, after the top 10 still looked very off after Open #4, I made the decision to make an adjustment to the RAE model.

The biggest change implemented was the movement to a new model for rating each of the regions, which weighs each matchup via the teams in it, rather than their domestic seeds. The end result is that major regions come out higher, and minor regions come out lower. The model is also more reactive now and punishes teams slightly less for missing the main event, which has lead to a top 10 that's much cleaner. Falcons are still stuck at #2, but they're basically tied with NRG, so I can deal with that.

RAE had an 83% accuracy in Open #4 and a 77% accuracy in Open #5. We'll see what the final regional of the season brings.

North America

Pretty standard stuff here: NRG on top, Ultimates behind them, Gen.G, SSG, and Complexity fighting for the top four, and the rest of the 6-11 bubble scrapping for a shot.

Let's go over some of the more controversial picks.

Who comes out on top in the race for fourth?

You probably don't need a predictive model to answer this question, but RAE at least helps put it into perspective how Ultimates should be the heavy favorite here.

They come out with about 70% odds to be the last team standing, followed by Complexity at 25%. The Boys and Strictly Business, despite strong performances in Open #5 (especially from the former), just don't have the talent to give either of the favorites a real challenge in the model's eyes.

The Boys will also have to fight through Shopify Rebellion and likely Deleted Gaming and 9Lives just to make day 2, so they'll need to prove that they're truly a step above the rest of the NA chase pack if they want to even put themselves into consideration.

Once again, NRG has all but locked up NA's #1 seed, and two teams (this time Spacestation and Gen.G) are all but locks to join them at the major.

In the four-way tie for the final spot between The Ultimates, Complexity Gaming, Strictly Business, and The Boys, RAE clearly favors Ultimates, with only Complexity having a realistic shot at pipping them for the last spot. It's a bigger struggle for The Boys and Strictly Business, who will each have to beat the odds in a tough Group B.

As for everyone else, including Evo, 9Lives, Deleted, and Shopify, despite being a stone's throw away from 4th, the sheer quality of Ultimates and Complexity makes their run a tall task. Do you (still) believe in miracles?

Middle East & North Africa

Team Falcons and Twisted Minds are still the clear top 2, but there's a much larger gap between the two teams than there was before Birmingham. Team Vision, Al Qadsiah, and ROC Esports have all made the giants bled; they'll be anxious for a chance to upset Twisted once again.

Will Twisted Minds miss the major?

It's not likely, but it's certainly more possible than it would've seemed just a few weeks ago.

After throwing away an all-but-guaranteed win over Falcons in the Open #4 grand finals, Twisted were still in a fairly comfortable position to make the major. After a group stage loss to OFF and a reverse sweep at the hands of ROC, though, questions have been raised about their quality.

The fact that it was ROC is at least some good news for the favorites — if Al Qadsiah or Team Vision had been the ones to make it to a grand final, their position would be in a lot more danger. Instead of sweating it out, they likely only need a semifinal berth to make it to Raleigh.

Still, it's a position that Twisted don't really want to be in. By the numbers, we've seen plenty of less probable events take place than the 9% shot that they miss LAN. We'll see if there's a few more upsets in the cards.

Oceania

OCE's four tiers are clear: Wildcard, the major chase pack, the top 8 bubble, and everybody else.

Will it be 2nd time lucky for PWR?

Though they didn't win an event this time, PWR are in a remarkably similar spot to the one that 2⁄3 of the squad found themselves in last time around.

Their odds are basically identical (47.6% vs 47.4%), as though RAE now sees them as the second best team in OCE, their points margin over 3rd is only 2 points compared to 3, so they absolutely need to make grand finals again to clinch a spot.

Chiefs, though thoroughly underwhelming, are still solid enough to take their spot. TSM is due for a breakout event of their own any moment now, too, so this spot is going to be the most competitive of them all, with a true three-way battle (and I'm fine in the west lurking if they can make a miracle run of their own) set to decide it.

Here's hoping that we can get some new OCE blood on LAN.

Sub-Saharan Africa

FUT Esports are a mile ahead of the pack, with only Limitless and Hey (with rizz) coming anywhere close to them in quality. le bosh, 77Blocks, and CosmiCo Esports have all shown upset potential at times.

Can FUT be competitive on the world stage?

Regardless of whether or not it's fair for them to qualify through a minor region (it's not), FUT's dominance is hard to ignore. They put up a respectable performance against Geekay and came within a game of taking down Team Secret and Wildcard.

Since they've already locked up their spot, their focus has likely already turned to Raleigh. The team is still well behind any team that will be competitive, but will be within striking distance (or even ahead) of OCE2. It's a shame that it's them, but the odds of an SSA team taking down another region without a GC1 on the roster is as high as its ever been.

Will Open #6 be the resurgence of SSA?

Surprise! It's the minor region with all of its LAN spot already clinched that gets the second question this week.

SSA is in need of a feel-good story, and if it were going to happen at any time, it'd be now. Seven majority-SSA teams made the main event this week, the highest total of the season. Even Unity and White Rabbit Gaming, who'd been languishing in Swiss after years of making main events, finally made their first one this past weekend.

To be frank, the odds are still fairly slim. The likelihood of a majority-native team winning the event is only 10%, and even if you take FUT out of the equation, "import" teams are still favored to win over the likes of Limitless and CosmiCo. Still, one can dream, right?

Also, can someone check in on Mustu Fluck? They were making top 48s in Europe last season, but missed the Swiss Stage entirely in the past three opens. They've dropped from SSA's second-best team to their ninth-best, losing nearly 300 rating points. Does anyone know what's up with them?
