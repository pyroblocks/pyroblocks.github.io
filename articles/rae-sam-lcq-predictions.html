RAE Predictions — 2025 SAM LCQ

Who will South America send to Lyon?

July 21, 2025

With the main RLCS 2025 circuit done and dusted, it's time for the best four regions to decide which four teams will secure their ticket to the World Championship in Lyon.

Second on the docket: SAM.

If you'd like an explanation of what exactly RAE is, you can find one here.

MENA LCQ Recap

Though things generally followed the script in MENA, RAE's fundamental assumptions (such as Vision being the top team in the event) were proven wrong, leaving it with an accuracy of just 76%.

ROC winning was never out of the question in the model's eyes, but the level of domination that they showed both led to a pair of incorrect predictions against Vision and a massive rating jump of +125.

Equally as damaging was Al Qadsiah's run — while the team wasn't expected to be bad, and got a fairly free run to the grand finals, they still were expected to be the fifth best team in the event. Obviously, that wasn't true.

Finally, of course, is the fact that both TRT and Synergy cratered in the quarterfinals. While the latter wasn't too surprising, the former were one of the event favorites (and in fact the favorites after day 1). Still, it was a decent performance for RAE. SAM might end up being a bigger test for it.

RAE Rankings — SAM LCQ

For those of you that haven't been following South American RLCS this season, what you see here has been the state of the region for most of the season: Behind FURIA and Secret, there is a strong 3rd–8th range, with each team being just a little bit better than the team below it.

The order isn't particularly surprising; Corinthians are probably better than Godfidence, who are probably better than Amethyst, and so on; the only really arguable placement here is TBS over Sunset, but the former has been suffering from terrible bracket luck this entire season.

Like in MENA, SAM has two teams that are in between the bubble and the contender pack, in this case being Overlooked and ShindeN, who both took on additions from Latino Heat. I'll touch more on this later, but besides for FOFUCHOS, we're really looking at the very bottom of the SAM bubble taking up the rest of the field, including a content creator team in os peixonautas.

Are TBS, Sunset, and Moonrise really that close to the top 3?

While SAM has had a clear 3rd–8th range, the consensus is that there's also been a clear separation between the 3rd–5th and 6th–8th teams. RAE disagrees, with the gap between Amethyst and TBS being smaller than that between Corinthians and Godfidence.

The culprit here is really Open #5: Before the event, Corinthians were almost clear of everyone else for the SAM3 spot. Afterwards, everybody else in the SAM top 8 gained besides for them, while they dropped down. Because both Amethyst and Godfidence beat them in the event, people began separating these three into their own tier. But it's also important to remember that both TBS and Sunset had almost equally-strong performances in Open #5, and Moonrise looked extremely strong in Open #4.

The top 3 teams in this event have a combined 72% chance to qualify to Lyon, but every team in the conversation has had a day where they've been the third best team in the region. We'll see if the LCQ follows the script.

Where are the SAM bubble teams?

When I first looked at the Swiss Stage for the SAM LCQ, I was puzzled by one thing in particular: there were only 13 or 14 teams with any recognizable players on them.

Missing were SAM bubble mainstays like RED, Papo de Visão, and LUK Esports. Taking their place? PJ and folly (aka Luk) coming back out of retirement, a full content creator team, and some players who had never placed above top 28. All of them made the top 16.

The teams placed 17th–32nd were very poor quality, with a couple of GC2s sneaking into Sunday. With only 86 teams participating (compared to over 200 in Open #6), it's no surprise that the depth bottomed out.

As retired bubble player krahyz put it, because there's no prize pool, a lot of bubble players feel like it's pointless to play if they won't win the entire event.

In any case, it's made for an essentially free run to the top 8 for a handful of teams, particularly Corinthians, who only have to beat pneu malandro esports and either True Neutral Black or "Sybau" to make it to playoffs through uppers.

Eighty6 drew the short end of the stick here, having to play Godfidence, then probably ShindeN, just to even make it to the top 12.
