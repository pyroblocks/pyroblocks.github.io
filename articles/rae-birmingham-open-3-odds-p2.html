RAE's Birmingham Open #3 and Major Odds (Week Two)

Which sixteen teams are set to qualify for the major?

February 25, 2024

Last weekend, Team Vitality, Dignitas, Geekay Esports, Team Secret, and Luminosity Gaming joined NRG, Karmine Corp, and FURIA Esports in qualifying for the RLCS 2025 Birmingham Major.

This weekend, just eight spots remain: Three in NA, two in MENA, two in OCE, and one in SSA.

If you'd like an explanation of what exactly RAE is, you can find one here.

EU/SAM/APAC Recap

Here's a brief summary of how the RAE model did in each region last week.

In Europe, RAE correctly predicted 3/4 major teams correctly, only missing on NiP (who missed out thanks to an improbable run by Geekay). The overall accuracy in the region was 73%, moreso due to 100% and Gentle Mates underperforming than Geekay, who the model rated fairly highly going into the event. Dignitas still feels a bit underrated by the model, but will likely keep rising as the season goes on.

SAM continued its streak of being the most predictable region, with RAE predicting 82% of matches correctly. The model also called Secret securing their spot at the major, though that wasn't really a surprise to anyone. After a hiccup against Fear of God, Godfidence slightly overperformed, and managed to put themselves in the driver's seat for the SAM3 spot ahead of Corinthians, though it's shaping up to be a fierce battle between the two sqauds.

It's a similar story in APAC, where Luminosity officially secured the region's one spot in Birmingham after Day 2. Prediction accuracy here was very low though (71%), as Overlooked took back-to-back upset losses to SENZA and KINOTROPE to go out in last. Executioners were also much better than expected, and took series off of God Speed and SENZA to make a surprise top 6.

Overall, it was a fairly poor week for RAE, as it had a total accuracy of 75%, a mark down from its 80% across the course of the season so far. I'm hoping that this week will be better.

North America

NRG has already clinched NA's #1 seed in Birmingham, and with Karmine's slip-up in EU, are looking to claim the title of #1 team in the world. They probably won't get there in RAE's eyes, though. The Ultimates and Complexity Gaming are scrapping for second, with 9Lives taking the #4 spot in the model's rankings before the rest of the ratings level off.

Let's go over some of the more controversial picks.

How is Spacestation only NA8?

I've been defend this roster's ranking since they made their first top 4 in 2024, so believe me when I tell you that, for the most part, this ranking makes sense.

The thing about Spacestation is that they're remarkably inconsistent. That much should be obvious: There's no better way to describe a team who nearly toppled NRG in the grand finals before going out in Swiss two weeks later. Inconsistency like this makes the ratings feel off, as they assume that every team is about as consistent as any other team in terms of how much their skill level can vary in any given match.

This problem gets even worse for SSG because they up and missed Open #2's GSL stage. In these ratings, missing a top 16 is about equivalent to losing a series and a half against a bubble team. It's not perfect methodology, but it's fairly solid, and in Spacestation's case, it caused them to lose about 40% of the 110 RAE gain they got from the first event.

This leaves the roster right in the middle of the pack in North America, which isn't too odd if you think about the fact that the average between a 2nd place and a top 22 is just about where the team lies.

Combine this with SSG starting as NA14 before Open #1, and you get their placement. If they're as good as people think, they will rise to a more accurate rating.

How badly did 9Lives get bracketed?

In short, pretty badly.

The full odds are below, but 9Lives getting NRG in the first round followed by likely Strictly Business and one of Shopify Rebellion and SSG just to make top 8 is a brutal run. Their position points-wise heading into the event was fairly strong, but having to run through this gauntlet just to get to Day 2 has weakened their odds of doing so to just over 50%.

If they can make it to playoffs, they'll be firmly in the driver's seat, but it's a very tough draw for a team that's played fairly well this split. Spacestation will be waiting in the wings to try to steal their spot if they slip up early, but the groups being this unbalanced has also opened up a small window for Rebellion and Deleted, who'd otherwise be dead in the water if it weren't for the realistic possibility that 9Lives misses top 8 and SSG missses top 6.

Middle East & North Africa

There's a pretty clear-cut hierarchy in MENA: Twisted Minds and Team Falcons, the chase pack (consisting of Al Qadsiah, MTS, BOV, ROC, ZNTRX [formerly AWAWA], and Infinity), and everybody else.

Can Twisted surpass Falcons with another event win?

In all likelihood, no, but it's not impossible.

Both teams have been dominant, but it's hard to understate just how unbeatable Falcons are to teams below them. They hardly drop a game in the group stages, and the only team to take two games off of them besides for Twisted is ROC. This means that they continue to make slow gains in rating throughout, while TM will mostly be looking for big wins over Falcons themselves to make gains.

That being said, a head-to-head victory, especially a 4-0 or 4-1, would probably be enough for Twisted Minds to surpass Falcons in RAE rating. You might as well pay attention to this while watching MENA this week, since there's not much else at stake.

Oceania

Wildcard is the clear top team, but Helfie Chiefs, TSM, and SKRIMZWORLD have shown the ability to take the fight to them. PWR, Oi Oi, Ground Zero Gaming, and Prodigy round out the top 8.

Can SKRIMZWORLD do it again?

The adam_baguette-lead SKRIMZWORLD roster was hovering around the OCE5 spot after the first event before breaking out in Open #2 and taking 1st place over Wildcard (PWR in 2024) for the first time since the 22-23 season. Were it not for Geekay's improbable run to the major, their event win would've been the biggest underdog run in 2025 thus far, as RAE gave them only 5% odds to topple the entirety of OCE's big three.

Beyond giving us a four-way brawl for OCE's two major spots, Skrimz also cemented themselves as a bona-fide top team in OCE. Teams that breakout with wins like this tend to stick around, but they'll need to be on their A-game in Open #3: Even a top 4 result will likely still require a tiebreaker victory over TSM or Chiefs to make it to Birmingham.

Sub-Saharan Africa

SSA has a pretty clear 1-2-3 in Maestros del Esférico, Limitless, and Hey (with rizz) with the rest of the region a step behind. SSA's depth is stronger than most regions' thanks to the constant influx of EU bubble players: The RAE gap between the #1 and #16 teams in the regional is just 438, compared to an average of 598 in the other three regions playing this weekend.

We need a 2/3 rule.

The median number of SSA teams in the top 8 from these simulations is two. Two. This is untenable. Please do something, Epic.
