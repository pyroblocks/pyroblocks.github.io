Raleigh Major Recap

The Raleigh Major was an upset-heavy event, and RAE's accuracy suffered as a result.

Overall, it secured an accuracy of 62%, its worst single-event performance of the season. Karmine Corp and FURIA, RAE's top two teams heading into the major, didn't make it past the top 8, and on their own contributed to a third of the model's missed matches.

Other underdog runs, such as Team Secret's march to the top 6 and Dignitas' unlikely-but-not-impossible run to the grand finals threw a wrench into the system.

I'd be remiss if i I didn't mention the complete collapse of North America, which followed up its great day 1 with an abysmal day 2. Losses by Ultimates, SSG, and GenG contributed to another quarter of the model's missed matches this event.

The current international rankings are... not great. I'll likely be playing around with things some time between the EU LCQ and Worlds.

Raleigh Major RAE Rankings

Although Vision and the old Al Qadsiah roster were about even before the latter replaced DrKnown with Rezears, the move has since placed Vision into a clear first place spot.

TRT and ROC check in at 2nd and 3rd, with Reds, The Vicious, Al Qadsiah, Synergy, and to some extent AWAWA hot on their tails.

The depth beyond the top 8 is pretty weak, with only Gold being within 150 rating of AWAWA for #8. It's worth noting, though, that because of the bracket, at least one team in this top 8 will not be making it to Saturday.

Are Vision really the favorites?

To say that the experienced roster of Team Vision are the favorites isn't exactly a hot take, but they've been in a fierce battle with ROC and Al Qadsiah all season over who's the best team to stand next to Falcons and Twisted at Worlds.

This is all to say that seeing RAE have them as fairly clear favorites might come as a bit of a surprise. The reasoning for this is simple, though: they've managed to avoid big slip-ups.

Take ROC: they're the only team to beat one of the MENA big 2 in playoffs this season, but that result was sandwiched by a pair of top 12s.

Al Qadsiah and TRT have been a little better, especially in Split 2, and the roster core has a dominant 4-1 head-to-head against Vision this season. But they just cannot beat ROC, who have crushed them 5-0 in RLCS 2025.

It's a bit of rock-paper-scissors in that sense, with ROC beating Al Qadsiah (and/or TRT; seriously, this is so confusing), Qadsiah beating Vision, and Vision usually beating ROC.

That "usually" carries some weight — Vision are only 2-1 against ROC this season, but both wins came in Split 2, and it's balanced out by ROC losing some extra series to worse teams as well. It's going to be seriously interesting.

Is TRT really better than Al Qadsiah?

RAE seems to think so.

The new Al Qadsiah roster is probably being a bit underrated — Nush's rating is from before he was benched in Split 1, and ops' comes from his time on NOM. So, yes, you can blame ntri a bit for this one.

To be clear, TRT did go down after the Rezears move, mostly because his rating is taken from a mediocre Chiefs roster in a terrible OCE this season, but both Tox and Toxiic get to carry over their 1700 ratings from the Split 2 roster, and thus this team stays in 2nd.

Yes, the showmatch win was impressive. Two unofficial Bo7s, however, do not determine team quality wholesale, especially when one of them is on holiday.

Nush is still nasty, though.
